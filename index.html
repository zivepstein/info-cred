Organizers

Maurice Jakesch

Maurice Jakesch is an Information Science PhD candidate at Cornell University. He’s a fellow at the Cornell Tech Digital Life Initiative and the German National Academic Foundation. Maurice studies how people reason about credibility and authenticity in a networked society. He holds a particular interest in epistemology, causality and system change that he seeks to incorporates into his credibility and authenticity research.

Manon Revel

Manon Revel is an Social and Engineering System PhD candidate at MIT and a Hammer Fellow. Manon studies news credibility and the trust crisis of journalism. She also works on political behaviors and electoral systems. She created and led webradios programs and is passionate about supporting and enhancing journalism’s quality.

Ziv Epstein

Ziv Epstein is a PhD student in the MIT Media Lab, where he is co-advised by David Rand and Sandy Pentland. His work integrates aspects of design and computational social science to understand and build cooperative systems. I focuses on new challenges and oppurtunities that emerge from a digital society, particularly in the domains of social media and artificial intelligence.
